## In Her Own Words (Themes from Interviews)

- **Origin story:** Daughter of immigrant parents (New Mexico). Career options framed as doctor, lawyer, engineer, concert pianist. First CS class at BYU — wrote a simple computer game — “flipped a switch” toward building software products. “The only thing stopping my success was me”; “great equalizer was how hard I worked.”
- **Career philosophy:** “Jungle gym” career — raised her hand for every opportunity, including lateral or “backward” moves (e.g. relocation to London for M&A integration). That range (developer, PM, architecture, integration, technical writing, engineering management) prepared her for VP-level at Adobe as one of the youngest VPs.
- **Mantra (from parents):** “Never be afraid to lose sight of the shore and discover new lands.”
- **Leadership:** Hire great people, align on strategy, remove roadblocks. Lead by influence more than authority. Multi-layered customer feedback. Product, design, and engineering in collaborative pods. “Dare to make mistakes” and learn from failure. “People will remember how you made them feel long after they remember what you said.”
- **Mentors:** Kevin Lynch (Apple/Adobe), Josh James (Omniture/Domo), Brett Error (Omniture CTO). Theme: customer success, innovative products, doing good.
- **Influences:** *Daring Greatly* (Brené Brown) — courage to be vulnerable.
- **Current relevance:** At Entrata she is driving AI-powered product (e.g. ELI Invoice Entry, AI/ML in workflows). Profile on AI Utah (aiutah.org) indicates intentional presence in the local AI ecosystem.
