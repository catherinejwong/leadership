# Catherine Wong — LinkedIn Style Guide

**Derived from:** R1 2026 press release and LinkedIn post development session (March 2026).
**Use this document** as the starting point for any future LinkedIn post drafts. It captures voice, framing decisions, and lessons learned so we don't re-litigate them.

---

## Voice and Tone

- **Builder-operator, not philosopher.** Show what you've built and what it means. Don't lecture about the future of work.
- **AI-native confidence.** Talk about AI capabilities the way Notion or Harvey does — as if this is obviously how it should work. Don't marvel at the technology.
- **Outcome-focused.** Describe what changed for the user, not what Entrata did to the product.
- **Direct, not corporate.** Write like you'd talk at a dinner, not how you'd write a press release. "I've been building enterprise SaaS and data platforms for 25 years" not "I've spent my career building enterprise SaaS, data, and analytics platforms."
- **The people are the hero.** The AI is infrastructure. The human transformation is foreground. Harvey's model: "legal work defined by expertise, not busywork." Ours: teams freed to do the work that drew them to this industry.

## Framing Rules (Settled Decisions)

### What Entrata Did with AI

| Don't say | Why | Say instead |
|---|---|---|
| "Embedded AI" | Positions AI as a commodity being inserted; every SaaS company says this | "The platform acts on what it knows" |
| "Added AI to the PMS" | Implies the PMS was static and AI is the new, external thing | "The PMS is agentic" |
| "Rebuilt the platform around AI" | Dismisses 20 years of operational intelligence; implies the old product was inadequate | "Twenty years of operational intelligence, now lit up with agents" |
| "Powered by AI" | Makes AI the hero; Entrata's domain intelligence is the hero | "Earned over two decades and millions of units" |
| "Unleashing AI" | Hyperbolic, vague, implies AI was caged | Avoid entirely |

### The Core Frame

The intelligence was always there — two decades of property management operations encoded in the platform. What changed is that those operations now execute themselves. AI is the mechanism; Entrata's operational intelligence is the raw material.

**Best single line:** "The platform acts on what it knows."

**Best single word for what happened:** "Lit up." The intelligence was wired; now it's on. Avoids "electrified" (overplayed AI-is-electricity analogy), "graduated" (ambiguous — gradual? promoted?), "activated" (generic).

### Governance and Compliance

- **Governance is a capability, not a disclaimer.** Don't say "while maintaining oversight and control" (defensive, validates the fear). Instead show that the system provides more visibility than manual operations ever could.
- **Don't reassure — upgrade.** The customer shouldn't feel relief that they still have control. They should feel excited that they finally have visibility.
- **The compliance flip:** The biggest compliance risk in property management was never AI. It was the gap between what policies say and what actually happens across thousands of interactions with no audit trail.
- **Two mentions is enough.** Governance themes can appear in the platform-intelligence paragraph (what the platform encodes) and the customer-ownership paragraph (whose rules govern the agents). Don't repeat it a third time in the people/outcome section — by then the reader trusts it.

### Customer References

| Don't say | Why | Say instead |
|---|---|---|
| "Our customers don't differentiate on X" | Tells them their strategy; presumptuous | "Our customers know what sets them apart" |
| "200 site teams interpreting policy manuals differently" | Insulting to customers who built those operations | Reserve for internal strategy docs, not public posts |
| "Not in a binder" | Implies customers are unsophisticated | "Encoded in the system of record" |
| "Our customers' workforce" (collective) | Sounds like Entrata controls one workforce | "Each customer's" or "their teams" |

### Agent Ownership

Agents belong to the customer, not to Entrata. Entrata's platform intelligence is the foundation. Each customer's SOPs, policies, and data direct how their agents operate. Structure paragraphs so that platform intelligence (Entrata's contribution) and customer authority (the customer's rules) are in separate sections, with the agent-as-workforce concept landing after customer ownership is established.

### Credibility and Career

- **Lead with technologist identity, not multifamily.** "I didn't come from multifamily" is a differentiator, not a weakness. Chase covers the industry-veteran angle.
- **25 years, not a list of companies.** "I've been building enterprise SaaS and data platforms for 25 years" is natural. Don't list Omniture, Adobe, Domo unless the format calls for it (bios, fireside intros).
- **Don't claim "multiple industries."** Omniture and Adobe are the same domain. Say "enterprise SaaS and data platforms" — that's the through-line and it's unchallengeable.
- **"I've never seen a product with this density of domain intelligence ready to be activated"** is the strongest credibility line. It leverages the outside-in perspective.

## Formatting Rules

- **No em-dashes.** They now read as AI-generated. Use periods for separation. Use commas for light pauses.
- **No more than ~250 words.** LinkedIn engagement drops sharply beyond this. If it needs to be longer, every paragraph must earn its place.
- **Short paragraphs.** One idea per paragraph. Many paragraphs will be a single sentence. This is LinkedIn, not a memo.
- **Three-beat lists for emotional payoff.** "The resident who needs connection. The portfolio decision that requires judgment. The living experience that turns a unit into a home." Ascending from individual to portfolio to purpose.
- **Avoid LinkedIn boilerplate.** No "What excites me most," no "I'm thrilled to announce," no "Let me share some thoughts on." Just start.
- **Close with action or anticipation, not summary.** "More tomorrow." or a tag. Don't restate the thesis at the end.
- **Specificity = confidence.** "110 agents" not "100+ agents." "25 years" not "decades." "3.2 million units" when the number is current; "millions of units" when cumulative over time.

## Audience Awareness (Multi-Reader Posts)

LinkedIn posts from the COO/CPO of a pre-IPO company are read by at least three audiences simultaneously:

1. **Customers and prospects** — need to see themselves in the post, not be talked about. Credit them with intelligence. "Our customers know what sets them apart" not "our customers need to focus on X."
2. **Investors and analysts** — read for signals. SoR/SoC/SoA can be implied through description without using the jargon. "Encoded in the system of record" and "available as real-time context" land for both audiences.
3. **Talent and tech community** — read for culture and leadership signal. The technologist credibility and honest-about-displacement stance serve this audience.

When in doubt, write for the customer and let the investor read between the lines.

## Relationship to Other Executives' Posts

- **Don't echo the CEO.** Catherine and Chase should be two halves of one story, not two versions of the same post. He lands vision and product boldness. She lands human impact, operational credibility, and governance.
- **Don't correct the CEO.** If his framing is different (e.g., "rebuilt"), offer a deeper version of the same point rather than a visible contradiction. "The intelligence was already there" complements "we rebuilt" without opposing it.
- **Rolling thunder sequencing:** CEO/President posts can be bolder and more product-forward. COO/CPO post should be the one customers trust — grounded, specific, human.

## Formula (from POSITIONING.md, refined)

**LinkedIn post = Core message + one pillar + a specific observation or product moment.**

- Core message doesn't need to be stated verbatim every time. It can be shown through the structure (AI handles load → people do meaningful work).
- Pillar 2 (honest about displacement) is the strongest for product announcements.
- Pillar 4 (career proof) works as a single credibility line, not a full paragraph.
- The specific observation is what makes each post unique. For R1 it was "the PMS is agentic." For future posts it should be equally concrete.
