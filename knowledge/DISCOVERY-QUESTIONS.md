## Part 1: Personal Leadership Brand

### Who you are as a leader

1. **In one sentence, how do you want to be described when you’re not in the room?**  
   (e.g. “The operator who turns data into decisions” or “The leader who builds platforms and people.”)
   The pro who drives outsized outcomes with a human touch and by innovating tech. Sees around corners.

2. **What are the 2–3 leadership strengths you want to be known for?**  
   (From your history: scaling product/engineering, M&A integration, leading by influence, building pods, failing fast. Which do you want to lead with?)
   Scaling product (which I believe is increasingly going to mean scaling revenue). Leading on the forefront of technology. Enabling humans to do more than they thought possible for the good of the world. 

3. **What’s the one thing you’re willing to be controversial or distinctive about?**  
   (A belief, a practice, or a stance that sets you apart in high tech or in AI.)
   You don't have to be an a**hole to lead effectively. You don't need to be rude.  You can craft an experience that is great for high-performers. 

4. **When you think of “personal brand,” what feels authentic vs. performative?**  
   What would you never do for visibility, and what would you lean into?
   Personal drama is something I hate surfacing.  Being politically vocal is something I'm allergic to. I would lean into fireside chats, and helming programs that scalably have impact.  Rather than throw a starfish back into the sea (which I will do if I can of course), I want to make it so the waves cover the starfishes regularly, or push the starfishes into the sea regularly if they choose, whatever. 

5. **Who are 1–2 executives (in or out of tech) whose brand you admire?**  
   What specifically do you want to emulate or avoid?
   I admire Elon Musk for his output and his bravery, although I do think the price he pays is high for that on the personal front.  I think Pat Grady is one of the smartest out there. I think Jensen Huang is very savvy in how he shows up, but also gets a lot of grace because of NVIDIA's performance. 

### Story and audience

   Immigrant family, "jungle gym", one of these is not like the others and that's actually a good thing.

7. **Who is the primary audience you want to influence in the next 12–24 months?**
