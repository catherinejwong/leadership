# Catherine Wong — Positioning Statement

---

## Core Message (short-form — bios, intros, social, sound bites)

> **"The best thing about AI isn't what it can do — it's what it frees people to do."**

---

## Expanded Narrative (talks, fireside chats, bylined pieces)

I've spent 25 years building products and teams across engineering, design, and product — from Omniture's IPO through Adobe's $1.8B acquisition, from taking Domo public to now leading operations and product at Entrata. The through-line? People are ingenious when you get out of their way. AI is the biggest "get out of the way" moment in history.

The best thing about AI isn't what it can do — it's what it frees people to do.

Let's be honest: AI will eliminate roles. Entire categories of work are going to disappear. But I've watched technology do this before — and every time, the people who leaned in found that what came next was more creative, more human, and more meaningful than what they left behind. AI is opening entirely new planes for human creativity. The leaders who see that will build the next great companies — and the next great communities. This is the biggest expansion of human possibility since the internet — if we lead it that way.

At Entrata, that's not theoretical. Our AI is replacing mundane work that no one wanted to do in the first place — and the result is property teams with more time for residents, for asset value, for the judgment calls that actually require a person. The pattern is the same everywhere: the roles that disappear were never the point. The work that remains is.

## Supporting Pillars

Use these to expand the core message depending on audience and context:

### 1. New planes for human creativity
*"AI is opening entirely new planes for human creativity. The leaders who see that will build the next great companies — and the next great communities."*

- Audience: Investors, boards, CEO peers
- Proof: Career spanning engineering → design → product → COO/CPO; patents in data visualization and analytics; built platforms (Adobe Experience Cloud foundations, Domo, Entrata AI) that put power in end users' hands
- Use when: Talking about the future of companies, org design, or the AI opportunity

### 2. Honest about displacement, clear-eyed about what comes next
*"AI will eliminate roles. Let's not pretend otherwise. But the roles that disappear were never the point — the work that remains is."*

- Audience: Next-gen talent, operators, property/vertical leaders, anyone allergic to corporate spin
- Proof: ELI+ at Entrata — replacing mundane invoice processing (work no one wanted) so property managers focus on residents and asset value; Domo's Mr. Roboto scanning data so humans focus on decisions
- Use when: Talking about applied AI, explaining what Entrata is doing, or whenever someone asks "but what about jobs?" — lead with honesty, land on possibility

### 3. Human possibility — if we lead it that way
*"The AI revolution isn't man vs. machine. It's the biggest expansion of human possibility since the internet — if we lead it that way."*

- Audience: Policymakers (e.g. Governor's Office), community leaders, broad audiences
- Proof: Women Tech Council board (founding supporter, 17+ years), Amplitude board, university advisory boards, "outsized outcomes with a human touch" operating style
- Use when: Speaking to mixed or non-technical audiences, framing responsibility without being preachy

### 4. The career proof (narrative anchor)
*"I've spent 25 years building products and teams across engineering, design, and product. The through-line? People are ingenious when you get out of their way. AI is the biggest 'get out of the way' moment in history."*

- Audience: Any — this is the opening/credibility move
- Proof: Software engineer → architect → director → VP (M&A, London relocation) → Adobe VP (youngest at the table) → Domo CPO/COO (IPO) → Entrata COO/CPO. "Jungle gym" career. Immigrant family. "One of these is not like the others — and that's a good thing."
- Use when: Opening a talk, fireside chat intro, long-form bio, or whenever you need to establish why you have the right to say this
